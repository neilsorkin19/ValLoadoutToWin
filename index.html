Valorant Win Probability Calculator - CMSC320 Final Project

Created by Neil Sorkin - Undergraduate Student at the University of Maryland in CMSC320 (Introduction to Data Science)

Currently, the calculation outputs out a random number, but in the real version of the app, I will use Tensorflow to train a model for the real expected win probabilities, and use this model's inference to generate the probabilities.

The dimensionality of this problem is very high, so I will probably need hundreds of thousands of games to train an accurate model. Additionally, I need to be able to retrain the data when there is a new patch as the guns and agents can change. So, I need persistent access to the Valorant API.

In the future, I may add other variables, like ultimate points / status, ability purchases, the ability to input KDA in the match for each player (an 0/20/0 Jett is not going to change the outcome very much with their Bladestorm), and ping. As this calculator stands, it is more useful from an esports caster point of view where you know the loadouts of both teams and can numerically evaluate the "smartness" of a buy. To a player in the game, you don't know a lot of the information required, but perhaps I could make the calculator guess what the enemy team will buy based on their bank and how optimal each loadout is for that team.
